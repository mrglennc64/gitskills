# Research Summarizer Skill Overview

The **research-summarizer** is a structured analysis tool for digesting documents users already possess—academic papers, articles, reports, and technical documentation. It operates distinctly from web-search-based research domains.

## Core Capabilities

The skill executes three primary workflows:

1. **Single Source Summary** — Applies source-type-specific frameworks (IMRAD for papers, claim-evidence-implication for articles) and scaffolds findings into structured briefs with thesis, key findings, methodology, limitations, and actionable takeaways.

2. **Multi-Source Comparison** — Builds matrices across 2-5 documents, identifying convergent findings, contested points, and evidence gaps before synthesizing recommendations.

3. **Citation Extraction** — Detects and reformats citations across five styles (APA 7, IEEE, Chicago, Harvard, MLA) with deduplication and source classification.

## Quality Framework

All sources receive four-dimension ratings—credibility, evidence strength, recency, and objectivity—producing overall assessments (strong, adequate, or weak).

## Key Safeguards

The skill flags undated sources, contradictions between documents, paywall restrictions, incomplete citations, and obsolete content proactively. Every claim must anchor to specific source locations; invented metadata is marked "not stated."
